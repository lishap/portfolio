High School Party Simulator For Home Schooled Boys is a 360° video comedy sketch created by Lisha Payne and Martin Herlihy.

In my Immersive Experiences course, we focused on designing artistic, meaningful worlds for virtual reality headsets. Everyone else in my class designed an experience using Unity and the Oculus. Instead, I wanted to create an experience that was accessibile and would poke fun at expensive virtual reality. Instead, I created a 360° video so that it could be viewed on any mobile or desktop computer. In the simulator, you experience a high school party as imagined by your local church pastor, including classic party games such as Beer Pong and Spin The Bottle. Eventually, Pastor Martin crashes and ultimately ruins the party with an innappropiate kiss. After screening this at the ITP/IMA Spring Show 2019 with Google Cardboard, I realized I needed to add a buffer for those to get situated with the headset. Additionally, I wanted to add a warning for the nonconsenual kiss that occurs.

I used Ricoh Theta to capture video and stitched/edited using Adobe Premiere. I created the graphic fx in Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.
