Gobyworld is a point-and-click adventure web game where you play as Goby, a piece of ABC (already been chewed) gum, in his quest to find freedom beyond the digestive system. Gobyworld is my senior capstone project within the Interactive Media Arts program at NYU Tisch.

Point-and-click adventure games were the popular genre in the 1970s, 1980s and 1990s, but now they are not! Games like Myst, Poptropica, Colossal Cave Adventure, Maniac Mansion and Pajama Sam are now outdated for our current generation of young people. But, I think they are still relevant now because if you know how to use a computer, you know how to play a point-and-click adventure game. The affordance of accesibility is built into the genre.

So, I made a digestible point-and-click for this new generation. It was important that it was free, hosted on the web and use single-verb mechanics (instead of text parser or fixed verb). I used the Adventure Game Studio engine (C++) to build the game. I used Aesprite pixel editor to draw and design the artwork. I used Dan Harmon's Story Structure 101 and Ron Gilbert's puzzle dependencies to write the story and puzzles. After playtesting among friends and the NYU Game Center, I learned I needed to add more interactivity, clearer initial instruction and more signposting throughout the game.
