Gobyworld is a point-and-click adventure web game where you play as Goby, a piece of ABC gum, in his quest to find freedom beyond the digestive system. Gobyworld is my senior capstone project within the Interactive Media Arts program at NYU Tisch.

Point-and-click adventure games were the popular genre in the 1970s,1980s and 1990s, but now they are not! Games like Myst, Poptropica, Colossal Cave Adventure, Maniac Mansion and Pajama Sam are now outdated for our current generation of young people.

But, I think they are relevant now because if you know how to use a computer, you know how to play a point-and-click adventure game. So, I made a digestible point-and-click for this new generation, which needed to be free, hosted on the web and use single-verb mechanics (instead of text parses or fixed verb). I used the Adventure Game Studio engine (C++) to build the game. Aesprite to draw and design the artwork. Dan Harmon's Story Structure 101 and puzzle dependencies to write the story and puzzles. After playtesting it among friends and the NYU Game Center, I learned I needed to add more interactivity like animation, and ease the learning curve like increasing the signposting and clearer initial instruction.
